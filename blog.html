Engaging in Revenge Trades? Discover Why it is an Unwise Practice.

Forex trading involves both winning and losing trades. However, many traders tend to take losses personally and react by engaging in revenge trading. This behavior is primarily driven by the fear of being wrong, and it usually occurs when a trader, after experiencing a frustrating loss, attempts to make up for it by being more aggressive in their next trades. Revenge trading can be dangerous for your account for two main reasons. First, it causes you to abandon your trading discipline, diverting your attention from your trading process and good risk management to focusing on making enough money to recover your losses through less thoughtful trades.

Trading Decisions made based on luck and emotions is not trading right

Revenge trading is akin to gambling, as it lacks a risk management plan and can deplete your account with every trade. It's a lose-lose situation because if you lose, you deepen your losses with an impulsive and poorly planned trade. If you win, it reinforces the belief that emotional trading works, and you're likely to repeat the same mistake. Revenge trading can manifest in various forms, but the most common is taking bigger and impulsive trades after experiencing a frustrating loss, hoping to recoup the losses quickly. For instance, Ian experienced a loss of $97 on a short USD/JPY trade, and instead of sticking to his trade idea, he adjusted his stop and waited for a reversal. Unfortunately, his adjusted stop got hit, and he ended up losing $250, more than double his initial risk. Similarly, Mika lost $50 on a trade that went her way, but she got stopped out. Feeling frustrated, she took on a more aggressive trade than she was used to and put $100 at risk, which was double her usual amount. However, fearing another loss, she cut her winner as soon as she "recovered" her $50. In both cases, Ian and Mika deviated from their usual strategies and disregarded their risk management plan in favor of avoiding losses. Fortunately, there are ways to recover from revenge trading, which we'll explore shortly.

Take a break and give yourself time to relax and clear your mind after a frustrating loss.

Take a break from trading-related activities and return only after you have recognized that losses are an inherent aspect of the game.

Record the factors that caused you to lose your trade.

Pinpointing the issues with your trade and directing your efforts towards refining your trading process can reduce the sensation that the market is working against you.

Make a record of the triggers and cues in your trading journal.

Do you tend to engage in revenge trading when you have substantial positions or experience unforeseen catalysts? Do you typically nibble on snacks, bite your nails, or vent your frustration on your pet before making such trades? Being aware of your triggers can assist you in avoiding the temptation to engage in additional revenge trades.

Have faith in your system!

If you have thoroughly tested your system and adhere to your trading plan without exception, then any losses incurred will not be a cause for significant concern as you are aware that the final figures will eventually work in your favor.

Implement risk management strategies in your trading.

By incorporating risk management as a routine practice, you can improve your trading discipline and reduce the likelihood of making impulsive trades. If you're not accustomed to it yet, start with strictly adhering to rules regarding position sizes and trade durations. Keep in mind that even the most consistently profitable traders experience rough trading days. It's all part of the game, after all. Avoid interpreting losses as a sign that the market is working against you. It does not concern itself with your emotions or the soundness of your trade ideas. As traders, our duty is to trade based on what we see, not what we believe. Set aside your ego and concentrate on executing one successful trade after another. Remember, it's not you; it's simply the nature of forex trading.
